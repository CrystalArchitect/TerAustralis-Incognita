# Red Dust to Rockets — The Pilbara Proposal

> **Status: advocacy proposal.** Nothing described here is agreed, funded, sited, permitted,
> or endorsed. **This project has no affiliation with, or endorsement from, SpaceX or any
> company, agency or government named here.** Where a commercial operator establishes
> facilities is entirely that operator's decision to make. This document argues only that
> Australia should be ready if such a decision comes, and that the groundwork is worth laying
> either way. Circulated in May 2026 under the earlier working name "Starbase Down Under."

## The Argument in One Paragraph

Australia currently exports raw material into other countries' space programs and imports the
finished capability back at a premium. The Pilbara already has the three things a
southern-hemisphere propellant and recovery hub would need — critical minerals at scale,
deep-water port infrastructure, and a favourable position for Indian Ocean recovery
operations. The proposal is that Australia move deliberately from supplier to participant:
process minerals onshore into propellant and components, build recovery capability on the
west coast, and keep sovereign control and Traditional Owner partnership structural rather
than decorative. The window for doing this on Australian terms is not permanently open.

## Why the Pilbara

| Factor | What's already there |
|--------|----------------------|
| **Critical minerals** | Australia is the world's largest lithium producer; the Pilbara is a working extraction region with established logistics |
| **Port infrastructure** | Port Hedland is one of the world's highest-tonnage deep-water ports, already built and operating |
| **Geography** | Southern-hemisphere position with Indian Ocean access suited to recovery operations |
| **Legal framework** | The Australia–US Technology Safeguards Agreement entered into force in July 2024 |
| **Existing defence footprint** | DARC Site 1 in Western Australia is delivering early tracking data for AUKUS partners, with full operational capability targeted for 2027 |

Australia's 2026 National Defence Strategy identifies space as a warfighting domain. The
argument here is that space domain *awareness* — which Australia now has — is a floor, not a
ceiling.

## What the Proposal Covers

- **Onshore processing** of critical minerals into methalox propellant and higher-value
  components, rather than exporting ore
- **Recovery infrastructure** on the west coast for Indian Ocean operations
- **A sovereignty-first governance model**, with Traditional Owner partnership established at
  the outset rather than negotiated after the fact
- **Alignment with existing policy** — AUKUS Pillar 2 and Australia's Critical Minerals
  Strategy — rather than requiring new frameworks
- **Phased development**, beginning with propellant and recovery, with sovereign launch
  capability as a later-stage question

## Where This Came From

The underlying strategic case was published in *SpaceNews* on 22 May 2026 as *Leveraging
AUKUS and southern geography: building Australia's dual-use space infrastructure for strategic
resilience*. That piece proposed a trilateral working group coordinating across Canberra,
Washington and London to move Australia from space domain awareness toward full-spectrum
southern launch, recovery and manufacturing capability.

Two press releases followed, in May 2026, calling for action on the Pilbara siting question.

**One point of clarity, stated plainly:** Elon Musk replied "Possibly" to a public post about
Australian Starship operations. That is a one-word reply on a social platform. It is not a
commitment, an assessment, a negotiation, or a signal of intent, and this document does not
treat it as one. It is noted here only because it was the occasion for the press releases, and
leaving it out would be a different kind of dishonesty.

## What Would Have to Be True

This proposal is not close to reality, and the honest list of what stands between here and
there is short but heavy:

- ⬜ **Commercial interest** — no operator has expressed intent to site anything in the Pilbara
- ⬜ **Traditional Owner consent** — no agreements exist; the proposal's own governance model
  requires these to come first, not last
- ⬜ **Environmental and regulatory approval** — not sought
- ⬜ **Capital** — no funding pathway identified
- ⬜ **Technical feasibility study** — the propellant and recovery economics have not been
  independently modelled

Under the [status ladder](GOVERNANCE.md) used across this repository, every line above is
Design. Nothing here has moved further, and this page will be corrected downward immediately
if it is ever found running ahead of reality.

## Why Publish It Anyway

Because the argument is worth making publicly whether or not it succeeds, and because advocacy
that hides its own uncertainty isn't advocacy — it's marketing. The case for Australian
sovereign capability in this domain does not depend on any one company's decision. If the
siting question resolves elsewhere, the minerals-to-propellant argument stands on its own.
