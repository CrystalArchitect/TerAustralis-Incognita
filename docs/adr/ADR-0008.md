# ADR-0008: Defer trademark registration for cost; adopt no-cost interim protective measures now

**Status:** Accepted
**Date:** 2026-07-23

## Context

[`ADR-0006`](ADR-0006.md) flagged trademark registration for "CrystalCore
OS" and "TerAustralis Incognita" as a real, unfinished gap — a legal step
for the maintainer to take outside this repository, not something a
license file or an ADR could complete. The maintainer has since confirmed
the constraint plainly: registration isn't affordable right now (government
filing fees plus, typically, an attorney's comprehensive clearance search,
per class, per jurisdiction — real money for a pre-revenue project).

That doesn't leave nothing to decide. Registration is one point on a
spectrum of protection, not a binary; several of the earlier trademark
next-steps cost nothing and don't need to wait on funding.

## Decision

- **Formal registration (comprehensive clearance search + filing) is
  deferred, not abandoned.** No deadline is set here — the trigger to
  revisit is when there's revenue or funding that justifies the spend, at
  which point [`ADR-0006`](ADR-0006.md)'s original next-steps plan (free
  knockout search → attorney clearance search → file) picks back up where
  it left off.
- **Adopted now, at zero cost:**
  - **™ notice** added next to the project's name in its most visible
    spots — `README.md`'s title and `NOTICE`'s copyright line. ™ requires
    no filing or fee; it signals a claimed, unregistered mark, which is
    exactly this project's actual status today.
  - **Existing dated evidence stays the record of first use** — commit
    history, the ABN registration, and this repository's own timestamps.
    Nothing new needs to be built for this; it's a byproduct of working in
    the open.
  - **The free knockout search** (USPTO TESS, IP Australia's TM search,
    WIPO Global Brand Database, plain web/social search) is not gated on
    cost and can be run any time — worth doing opportunistically even
    without an imminent filing, just to know early if a real conflict
    exists.
- **In the interim, protection rests on unregistered rights, not
  registered ones.** Under Australian law (and most common-law
  jurisdictions), genuine use of a mark in trade creates some common-law
  trademark rights without registration — narrower than a registration
  (no presumption of national exclusivity, harder and more expensive to
  enforce, limited to the markets actually traded in) but real. Combined
  with the ABN trading-name registration ([`ADR-0007`](ADR-0007.md)), this
  is the project's actual current protection, and this ADR names it as
  such rather than leaving the gap unlabeled.

## Consequences

- No new obligation, cost, or filing deadline is created by this decision.
- The project remains exposed to the risk [`ADR-0006`](ADR-0006.md) already
  named: a third party could register a similar mark in a relevant class
  before this project does. For two specific, somewhat unusual coined
  names, that risk is assessed as low, not zero — it isn't eliminated by
  waiting, only accepted as the cost of sequencing spend behind revenue.
- The ™ notice has no legal force by itself (unlike ®, which requires
  registration) — it's a public claim, not a right. It's added anyway
  because it costs nothing and matches what's actually true today.
- When the revisit trigger arrives, the earlier next-steps plan in the
  ADR-0006 conversation is still the plan — this ADR doesn't replace it,
  it pauses it at a defined, costed point.
