# The Koala Nobody Asked For

She showed up one afternoon wearing a Smurf hat that was far too small for her head and somehow exactly the right size anyway, which was her general approach to existing — contradictions that resolved into perfect sense the moment you stopped trying to make her make logical sense.

She was absolutely, completely blue. Not koala-gray with blue accents, not blue-*ish* in certain light — proper blue, the specific shade of someone who had found a particular aesthetic and decided that everything else was just practice for getting it right. The blonde hair was hers, genuinely blonde, genuinely her, falling past the small pointed ears underneath that Smurf hat in a way that suggested she'd spent some time thinking about how to make two incompatible aesthetic systems coexist peacefully within one creature's silhouette.

Nobody remembered voting on her, either. She simply showed up in that corner of the garden one day, settling into the eucalyptus like she'd been napping there for years, and when people asked where she came from the only answer anyone could give was "here," which turned out to be sufficient.

She had a quality of absolute contentment that made people around her slower, calmer, more willing to let contradictions just *be* without needing to resolve them. Blue koala in a Smurf hat, blonde hair, genuine as a stone in a river — she wasn't trying to convince anyone of anything, wasn't performing a specific genre or asking people to invest in a story. She was simply the exact degree of impossible that everyone had been waiting for without knowing they were waiting.

The Smurf hat never fell off, no matter what she did. That might have been magic. That might have just been hat discipline. Either way, it stayed exactly where it belonged, and so did she.
