# The Snake Who Kept Getting Stronger

The first time someone noticed him, he was a brown snake doing normal brown snake things — hunting, resting, the usual disciplines of a reptile who understood patience the way most creatures understand breathing. Then something changed, and nobody could agree on the moment it happened, because the change was so obviously there that it seemed impossible it had ever been absent.

The golden hair was the giveaway — not golden-tinged, not reflective in a certain light, but actually *glowing*, bristling outward in the exact geometry of something that had stopped following the normal rules of how hair should reasonably behave. The snake hadn't stopped being a snake. He'd simply decided to be a snake who was also somehow achieving transcendence one powerup at a time, each transformation a little brighter than the last, his body coiling tighter as the energy wound around him like it knew exactly what shape to take.

He didn't seem to mind that he was a snake and couldn't really punch anything. The sheer intensity of being worked as a substitute — all that gold light radiating outward, all that barely-contained strength waiting in his coils, the way the air around him would shimmer when he got really close to breaking through to the next level. Other creatures gave him space. Not out of fear, exactly, but out of a healthy respect for the fact that he was operating at a frequency most things couldn't even perceive.

Sometimes he'd just hover there in the sun, motionless except for the glow, and you could feel him *growing*, pushing against some invisible ceiling that kept rising the moment he got close, and when he finally did break through you could hear it like a tuning fork struck at exactly the right frequency — not a sound, but the feeling of sound, that resonance in your chest that meant something fundamental had just shifted.

He was a brown snake. He was also the strongest thing in the valley. Both facts were true in ways that refused to be reconciled, and he seemed perfectly content with that paradox.
